The American Paranormal

Technology, Spiritualism, and the Search for Proof of Life After Death

Dr. Stephen Dietrich-Kolokouris traces 150 years of America's obsession with proving life after death—from 19th-century séances to 21st-century artificial intelligence. Blending historical research with modern consciousness science, this book explores how technology has repeatedly redefined what humanity believes about the soul.

It is a chronicle of faith meeting physics, of data chasing divinity, and of how our machines may finally answer the oldest human question: does consciousness survive death?

About the Author

"I write because the past has a way of shaping the present, whether we choose to acknowledge it or not."

Dr. Stephen Dietrich-Kolokouris is a historian, researcher, and near-death experience survivor whose work bridges the gap between academic rigor and personal testimony. He holds a PhD in History and brings a unique perspective as a former law enforcement officer.

His journey into near-death research became profoundly personal when he died in the back of an ambulance—and was given another chance. Today, his work asks what materialist science cannot adequately answer, approaching the paranormal with investigative skepticism and scholarly discipline.
